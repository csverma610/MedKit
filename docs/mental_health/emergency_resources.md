## When to Use Emergency Resources

### Call or Text 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)

**When to use:**
- Having thoughts of suicide
- Thinking about hurting yourself
- Feeling hopeless or overwhelmed
- In mental health crisis
- Need to talk to someone immediately
- Don't know what to do

**What to expect:**
- Trained counselor answers
- Free and confidential
- Available 24/7, 365 days
- No judgment
- Help finding local resources

**How to use:**
- Call 988 (like 911 for mental health)
- Text 988 (if you prefer texting)
- Available from any phone
- No need for minutes or service plan

**Language support:**
- Spanish speakers: Press 2
- Other languages: Press 3
- TTY: Use relay services

---

### Text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)

**When to use:**
- Prefer texting to talking
- In crisis but not safe to call
- Need support but can't talk
- Want to keep conversation private

**What to expect:**
- Crisis counselor responds
- Free and confidential
- Available 24/7, 365 days
- SMS text message conversation
- Help and resources provided

**How to use:**
- Text HOME to 741741
- Wait for response (may take a few minutes)
- Be honest about what's happening
- Follow counselor's guidance

---

### Call 911 or Go to Emergency Room

**When to use immediately:**
- Immediate danger to self or others
- Suicidal with specific plan and means
- Severely hurt/injured
- Unable to care for self
- Experiencing psychosis (hallucinations, delusions)
- Severe intoxication or overdose
- Medical emergency related to mental health

**What happens:**
- Emergency responders arrive
- Assessment of safety
- Transport to hospital if needed
- Psychiatric evaluation
- Safety planning
- Connection to treatment

**Don't hesitate:** If you're not sure, call 911. Better safe than sorry.

---

### Tell Someone You Trust

**Tell immediately:**
- Family member
- Close friend
- Therapist or counselor
- Teacher or school counselor
- Employer or manager
- Coach or mentor
- Anyone who cares about you

**Why important:**
- Creates accountability and support
- Someone knows where you are
- Can help get you to safety
- Provides emotional support
- Can advocate for you in crisis

## What Happens After You Call/Text

**Immediate (During Call):**
- Counselor assesses safety
- Develops immediate safety plan
- Provides coping strategies
- Listens without judgment
- Helps you feel less alone

**Short-term (24-72 hours):**
- Crisis stabilization
- Medical evaluation if needed
- Psychiatric assessment
- Safety planning
- Connection to local resources

**Follow-up:**
- Appointment with mental health professional
- Connection to ongoing therapy/counseling
- Medication evaluation if appropriate
- Support group information
- Discharge planning if hospitalized

## Creating a Safety Plan

**Write down:**
1. Warning signs that crisis may occur
2. Internal coping strategies (breathing, grounding)
3. People/social settings that help
4. Mental health professionals or therapists
5. Crisis resources (988, Crisis Text Line)
6. Ways to make environment safer
7. Reasons for living

**Keep it:**
- Easily accessible
- With you or memorized
- Updated as needed
- Shared with trusted people

## Warning Signs of Mental Health Crisis

**Seek help immediately if:**
- Thoughts of suicide or self-harm
- Talking about wanting to die
- Giving away possessions
- Expressing unbearable pain
- Seeing/hearing things others don't
- Severely withdrawn from people
- Reckless behavior
- Extreme mood changes
- Inability to function (self-care, work, school)
- Substance abuse increasing

---

## For People Around Someone in Crisis

**If someone tells you they're suicidal:**
- Take it seriously
- Don't debate or minimize
- Don't judge or shame
- Call 988 or 911 if immediate danger
- Stay with them if possible
- Remove access to means (drugs, weapons, etc.)
- Listen without trying to "fix" them
- Connect them to professional help
- Take care of yourself (get support)

## Myths About Mental Health Crises

**Myth:** Talking about suicide makes it more likely
**Fact:** Talking about it reduces risk. Ask directly.

**Myth:** Mental health crises are weakness
**Fact:** They're medical emergencies, not character flaws.

**Myth:** You have to be diagnosed to get help
**Fact:** Crisis resources help anyone in crisis.

**Myth:** Help is too expensive
**Fact:** Crisis resources are free; payment-based help available too.

**Myth:** Getting help means you'll be hospitalized
**Fact:** Most crises are managed as outpatient; hospitalization only if necessary for safety.

## After Crisis: Long-term Support

**Professional help:**
- Therapist/counselor (talk therapy)
- Psychiatrist (medication)
- Both combined (often most effective)

**Support groups:**
- Groups for specific conditions
- Peer support from others with same experience
- Often free or low cost
- Meetings in-person or online

**Lifestyle changes:**
- Sleep: Consistent sleep schedule
- Exercise: Regular physical activity
- Social connection: Time with supportive people
- Purpose: Meaningful activities
- Substances: Reduce/eliminate alcohol and drugs

**Medication:**
- If recommended by doctor
- Time to find right medication (may try several)
- Regular monitoring
- Don't stop abruptly without doctor approval
