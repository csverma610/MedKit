## When in Crisis

**If you have thoughts of suicide:**
- Call 988 (US) immediately
- Text HOME to 741741
- Call 911
- Go to nearest emergency room
- Tell someone you trust

**Don't wait. Don't minimize. Get help now.**

## Common Questions

**Is mental health screening accurate?**
Screening is starting point; professional evaluation confirms diagnosis.

**Will screening results be shared?**
Results are confidential; only shared if you consent or in safety emergency.

**What if I'm worried about a family member?**
Encourage them to get help; can't force adult to get treatment.

**How do I find a mental health professional?**
Ask primary care doctor for referral; check insurance; use directory services.

**Is therapy really helpful?**
Research shows therapy effective for most mental health conditions.

**What if medication doesn't work?**
Different medications work for different people; doctor can adjust or try others.

**How long does treatment take?**
Varies; some conditions resolve in weeks/months; others need longer-term treatment.

**Is mental health treatment expensive?**
Costs vary; insurance often covers; sliding-scale options available; crisis help is free.

## Resources for Different Situations

**Suicidal thoughts:** 988, Crisis Text Line, 911, ER

**Severe anxiety/panic:** 988, Crisis Text Line, ER if severe

**Depression:** Therapist, psychiatrist, primary care doctor

**Relationship problems:** Couples therapist, individual therapist

**Substance use:** Addiction specialist, support groups, rehabilitation programs

**Trauma:** Trauma-specialized therapist, EMDR or CBT

**Grief:** Grief counselor, support groups, therapist

**Work stress:** Employee assistance program (EAP), therapist

**Teen mental health:** School counselor, pediatrician, therapist specializing in teens

## Self-Care While Waiting for Professional Help

**Daily habits:**
- Sleep 7-9 hours
- Eat regular, nutritious meals
- Move your body (exercise helps mood)
- Spend time in sunlight
- Connect with supportive people
- Limit alcohol and drugs
- Practice relaxation (breathing, meditation)

**Healthy coping:**
- Journaling thoughts/feelings
- Creative expression (art, music, writing)
- Physical activity (walking, yoga, sports)
- Time in nature
- Helping others
- Engaging in hobbies
- Talking to trusted people

**Avoid:**
- Isolation
- Excessive alcohol or drugs
- Skipping meals
- Sleep deprivation
- Ruminating (thinking in circles)
- Negative self-talk

## For Those Supporting Someone

**Be supportive:**
- Listen without judgment
- Validate their feelings
- Avoid minimizing their pain
- Encourage professional help
- Respect their journey

**Know your limits:**
- You can't "fix" them
- Set healthy boundaries
- Take care of yourself
- Get support if needed
- Don't blame yourself

**Crisis support:**
- Take suicidal statements seriously
- Call 988 or 911 if in danger
- Stay with them if possible
- Remove access to means
- Connect to professional help
